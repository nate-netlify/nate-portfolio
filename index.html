About

I’m a driven creative with a background in video and marketing, as well as a knack for brand growth. I launched and developed a brand of my own in post-production, videography, and social management, specifically in the sports and esports realms, working with verified content creators & athletes, and high-profile organizations for a combined audience of well over a million. I currently shoot and edit content for athletes at all levels, and I’m passionate about video production in various arenas.

Still in the Paint

NWPC Finals

This recap, commissioned by Brown University for their water polo team's championship showdown versus Princeton, takes a more cinematic, "mini-movie" style approach. Its focus was framing, with particular attention being paid to depth of field and hero-style shots (November 2024).

Ivy Opener

This piece, for which I captured Cornell's Ivy League opener (and win over Columbia), involved some subtle but impactful VFX elements, including a title card and a 3D-tracked text animation. It was shot in S-Log3 and color graded in Davinci Resolve to give a softer look that fit gym lighting, and delivered a more artistic feel (January 2025).

Take Me Home Tonight

My first hockey game, this recap of Cornell's Ivy League-winning effort over Harvard was carefully crafted to a vintage track. Many of the shots were captured with a Tiffen Starburst filter, which added a layer of depth to the already complex grade (January 2025).

Suit and Tie

My first project on my new Sony A7SIII, this recap of Cornell Basketball's dominant win over Army focused on interesting pre-game shots & bench/crowd reactions. It was all tied together over top a classic but unique track for a sports videography piece (December 2024).
